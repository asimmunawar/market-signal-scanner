Scanner

Launch current scans or historical backtests from the same config file.

What to do hereRun a scan first. It ranks your configured tickers, then the Opportunity Map and Guardrails turn that scan into easier decisions.
Current scanBest for “what looks interesting or risky today?”
BacktestBest for “would these rules have behaved reasonably in the past?”

Current Scan

Runs the scanner on the tickers and groups in config/config.yaml, computes current signals, ranks assets, and writes buy/sell candidate reports.

Writes to output/scans/<timestamp>/.

Backtest

Replays the scoring rules over historical data using the backtest section in config/config.yaml, then summarizes simulated performance, trades, costs, and benchmark comparison.

Writes to output/backtests/<timestamp>/.

Interactive Chart

Explore ticker price action with zoom, pan, crosshair, overlays, and technical panels.

Novice readingUse charts to understand trend and risk. Do not buy only because a line is going up.
OverlaysMoving averages and levels are visual guides, not guarantees.
ConfirmationUse News Summary or Agent to explain why the chart moved.
Chart workflow: Start with 1Y or 2Y daily bars, hide extra overlays if the chart feels crowded, then switch to box zoom for a closer look at the decision area.

News Summary

Summarize a ticker using market signals, fundamentals, configured news sources, and a local LLM.

Use this forUnderstanding why a ticker moved and whether recent news changes the thesis.
Good habitCheck source dates. Old news should not drive today's decision.
WarningNews can explain attention, but it does not guarantee future returns.
Best use: Run this before buying a ticker from the scanner, after earnings, or when a position suddenly moves more than usual.

Writes to output/news/<timestamp>_<TICKER>/. LLM and news source settings live in config/config.yaml.

Market Agent

Ask anything about markets, stocks, crypto, macro, sectors, risks, catalysts, valuation, or timing.

Use this forQuestions that need source-grounded research, not quick chart reading.
Ask betterInclude your time horizon, risk tolerance, and what you already own when relevant.
Trust but verifyRead the sources and timestamps before acting.
Good question pattern: “I am a small long-term investor. Should I research TICKER now, wait, or avoid chasing? Separate technical, fundamental, and news evidence.”

Trend Catcher

Scan broad markets for major disruptions, sudden trend shifts, and high-attention opportunities.

What it doesLooks for fresh broad-market catalysts before starting from your favorite ticker.
What to doIf it flags something, decide whether to watch, research, avoid chasing, or review exposure.
Best useRun it when you want a market pulse, not when you need a full thesis.
WatchInteresting, but wait for confirmation.
ResearchEnough evidence to run Agent or News Summary.
Avoid chasingHigh attention, stale sources, or weak evidence.

Writes to output/trend-catcher/<timestamp>/. Trend Catcher is designed for quick 10-15 minute market checks and should only alert when attention is warranted.
